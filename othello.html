The board game that would become Othello was invented in the 19th century under the name Reversi. Sources conflict on whether the game was created by John W. Mollett, Lewis Waterman or by another individual even earlier. The game was rechristened "Othello" in the 1970s by Goro Hasegawa and marketed by the Japanese game company Tsukuda Original, then subsequently introduced to the American market by Pressman.A two-player game, Othello is quite simple to learn, but can take a lifetime to master.
